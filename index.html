ZEBA AFROZ

Research Scholar  |  FLaMe Lab, IIIT-Delhi

Hello! I am Zeba Afroz, a Research Scholar at the FLaMe Research Lab at IIIT-Delhi, India, supervised by Dr. Md. Shad Akhtar. My research lies at the intersection of Natural Language Processing (NLP), computational journalism, and social computing.

My work focuses on understanding how language is used to shape narratives — whether through ideological framing in news media, incongruences in eyewitness accounts, or figurative expression in online content. I am particularly interested in building robust AI systems that can reason about bias, deception, and narrative intent in real-world text.

I completed my M.Tech. from J.C. Bose University of Science and Technology, YMCA and my B.Tech. from Punjab Technical University. I describe myself as a self-motivated, positive, and result-oriented individual who enjoys tackling hard, meaningful problems.

Thank you for visiting my website! I am always open to collaborations in the areas of NLP and computational social science. Feel free to reach out if you'd like to discuss research ideas or potential collaborations.

2025

1.
Fine-grained Narrative Classification in Biased News Articles
Zeba Afroz, Harsh Vardhan, Pawan Bhakuni, Aanchal Punia, Rajdeep Kumar, Md. Shad Akhtar
LREC 2026
Proposes a novel fine-grained narrative classification framework for biased news articles, with article-bias classification as a precursor task alongside fine-grained persuasive technique identification. Introduces INDI-PROP — the first ideologically grounded fine-grained narrative dataset with multi-level annotation for propaganda in Indian news, covering 1,266 articles across two polarizing socio-political events (CAA and the Farmers' protest), annotated at three hierarchical levels.
arXiv Paper
2.
Incongruence Identification in Eyewitness Testimony
Akshara Nair, Zeba Afroz, Md. Shad Akhtar
arXiv Preprint, 2025
Introduces a novel computational task of detecting incongruences between eyewitness testimonies. Given Q&A exchange pairs from two subjects, the system identifies contextually related inconsistencies and marks their spans. Introduces MIND (MultI-EyewitNess Deception) — a dataset of 2,927 contextually related answer pairs — and the INTEND framework, grounded in investigative 6W and multi-hop reasoning.
arXiv Paper
